# Phase 3 — Demo (about 15 minutes)

You built a real AI agent. Last step: show it on the big screen. Keep it short,
honest, and live — a real reply in your voice is the whole point.

---

## Set up a "wow" moment (do this before you go up)

The best demos show the agent **doing something**, not just chatting. Pick one
and pre-load it so it lands on stage:

- 🧠 **Memory:** earlier in a chat, tell it a fact about you. On stage, open a
  fresh chat and ask it to recall that fact. (It remembers!)
- ⏰ **Reminder:** before you walk up, schedule a message for ~1 minute into your
  demo — so it pops up live while you're talking.
- 📧 **Email:** have it draft a reply to a real email and show how it sounds like you.

---

## Get on the big screen

1. Connect your laptop to the screen/projector (HDMI or whatever's provided).
2. Have your agent **already running** and open in the browser, full-screen.
3. Make the text bigger so the room can read it (zoom in: **Cmd/Ctrl** and **+**).

> Test the screen connection before it's your turn. Have your first message ready
> to type.

---

## The 3-minute demo

Keep it to about a minute of talking + showing:

```
0:00–0:30   "Hi, I'm [you]. This is [agent name] — it's an AI version of me."
0:30–0:45   "It [what it does], for [who — usually: me]."
0:45–2:30   Show it LIVE: send a real message, let the room see the reply.
            Then show your wow moment (memory / reminder / email).
2:30–3:00   "What I'd add next: [one idea]." Done.
```

**Tips**
- Show the real thing — don't read a script or fake it.
- Have a backup line if something lags: *"while it thinks — here's what it's doing…"*
- If something breaks, stay calm and talk about what you built. Everyone here
  knows it's a few-hour build.
